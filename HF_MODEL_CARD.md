# quark — quantum circuit embeddings

A small transformer (~647k parameters) that maps quantum circuits to L2-normalized 128-dim embeddings such that equivalent circuits have similar embeddings.

Trained with InfoNCE contrastive loss on synthetic (anchor, positive) pairs where positives are generated by composing nine equivalence-preserving rewrites (identity insert/cancel, disjoint-gate commutation, R_z split/merge, named-gate ↔ rotation, Clifford conjugation, gate algebra, Pauli-through-CNOT). Each anchor also gets a hard negative — a near-identical twin with one gate swapped for its inverse — so the model learns to tell a gate from its inverse.

## use

```python
import torch
from qiskit import QuantumCircuit
from quark.enc import CircuitEncoder, embed

m = CircuitEncoder()
m.load_state_dict(torch.load("quark.pt", map_location="cpu", weights_only=True))

a = QuantumCircuit(3); a.h(0); a.cx(0, 1); a.cx(1, 2)
b = QuantumCircuit(3); b.h(0); b.h(0); b.h(0); b.cx(0, 1); b.cx(1, 2)

ea, eb = embed(m, [a, b])
sim = (ea * eb).sum().item()
print(f"similarity: {sim:.4f}")  # around 0.96, versus ~0 for unrelated circuits
```

## numbers

Retrieval evaluation. Each query has 2 known-equivalent circuits hidden in a library of 500. Recall@k = "did at least one equivalent land in the top k."

### in-distribution synthetic

| method | R@1 | R@5 | R@10 |
|---|---|---|---|
| md5 hash of gate sequence | 0.035 | 0.035 | 0.035 |
| md5 hash of sorted gates | 0.080 | 0.080 | 0.080 |
| gate-count vector (cosine) | 0.410 | 0.825 | 0.880 |
| **quark** | **0.500** | **1.000** | **1.000** |

### held-out rewrite (model trained without insert_id, eval on insert_id-only pairs)

| method | R@1 | R@5 | R@10 |
|---|---|---|---|
| hash | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| sorted | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| count vec | 0.135 | 0.390 | 0.550 |
| **quark** | **0.470** | **0.970** | **0.985** |

The model generalizes to a rewrite type it never saw in training (R@10 = 0.985 vs 0.550 for the strongest baseline).

### QASMBench small (find transpiled lowering of an algorithm given its original form)

| method | R@1 | R@5 | R@10 |
|---|---|---|---|
| hash | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| sorted | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| count vec | 0.000 | 0.069 | 0.172 |
| **quark** | **0.000** | **0.103** | 0.172 |

29 algorithm pairs, library of 58. This is strongly out-of-distribution and the model's weakest result: quark edges the baseline at R@5 (0.103 vs 0.069) but only ties it at R@10. The hard-negative training that fixed the gate/inverse false positives (see discrimination below) lowered this from ~0.345 in earlier versions — separating near-twins and matching transpiled forms pull in opposite directions.

### discrimination (gate vs its inverse — these are NOT equivalent; lower is better)

| model | mean cosine | separated (< 0.9) |
|---|---|---|
| earlier versions | ~1.00 | ~0% |
| **quark** | **0.77** | **67%** |

Circuits differing only by a gate and a confusable sibling — an inverse (S/S†, T/T†) or a partner gate (CX/CY) — used to embed identically (cosine 1.0000, a confident false positive). They are now pushed apart (about −0.7 on the minimal S/S† pair) while genuine equivalences stay high (~0.96).

## limitations

- Training distribution: random gate sequences, 2-4 qubits, depth 8-24. Real-world circuits (QAOA, VQE, QFT) are likely out-of-distribution and may require finetuning.
- Equivalence is unitary equivalence up to global phase. Measurement-conditioned and classical-control circuits are out of scope.
- Encoder vocabulary covers 17 named gates plus 2 fall-through buckets. Exotic gate sets may degrade.
- Hard-negative training trades off against out-of-distribution recall: it fixed gate/inverse false positives but lowered QASMBench R@10 from ~0.345 to 0.172.
- The model is a heuristic similarity estimator, not a verifier. For exact equivalence use `quark.verify.equiv`, which computes the full unitary and is exponential.

## training data

Generated synthetically. Each anchor is a random 2-4 qubit circuit of depth 8-24 with gates sampled from {H, X, Y, Z, S, S†, T, T†, SX, R_x, R_y, R_z, CX, CY, CZ, SWAP}. Each positive is the anchor after a chain of 4 randomly-chosen equivalence-preserving rewrites; each anchor also gets a verified non-equivalent twin used as a hard negative. Rewrites are verified with `qiskit.quantum_info.Operator` to confirm they preserve unitary semantics.

## architecture

- 20-token gate vocabulary (h/x/y/z/s/sdg/t/tdg/sx/sxdg/rx/ry/rz/cx/cy/cz/swap + 2 fall-throughs + PAD); a gate and its inverse get distinct ids
- Token = sum(gate_emb, q1_emb, q2_emb, param_proj)
- Prepend [CLS]
- 3 transformer encoder layers, d=128, 4 heads, GELU
- L2-normalize the [CLS] hidden state

## ethical considerations

This is a research artifact. Outputs may be miscalibrated on out-of-distribution circuits. Do not use as a final authority on circuit equivalence — always verify with full-unitary comparison or a formal-methods checker for sensitive applications.
